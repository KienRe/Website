About Me
Hello there, my name is Rene Kienauer and I love making games. Since I can remember I have always been playing and talking about games. Even though I wanted to be a game progammer since I was 10 I did not really pursue this dream until much later in life. Now I have found my calling and cant stop thinking about programming and designing them!

When I am not dreaming up solutions for problems (programming) or dreaming up problems to solve (game design) then I am mostly watching all kinds of media I can get my hands on like good movies, lots of Youtube and different Twitch streams. I also do a lot of reading on Reddit and Twitter, probably much more than I should. Sometimes even you can find me on the outdoors reaching the top of an mountain or going for a run. I also enjoy politics, psychology, boats and the occasional party.

Programming

I have been programming now seriously for about three years before that I made some first experiences in the higher technical college I visited. I love the feeling of figuring out solutions to problems and then see them work in reality. Even though there is still much to learn for me I have the strong feeling that given enough time, no matter how hard the problem may seem at first, I can arrive at an working solution!

Mathematics

Unity

In the last two years I learned the in and outs of game development with the Unity Engine. Now after two years of using the engine I am confident to say that I am very comfortable with everything Unity related and I am able to build any kind of game I want inside the engine.

Game Design

I fell in love with game design at the SAE Institute just because no one else would do it for you. Slowly I failed my way upward, project per project until suddenly the games I made could be interpreted as fun. There is something very fascinating about having an initial idea and then iterating on it until someone has fun playing it. SAE Institute allowed me to do severall protoypes, game jams and even one physical card game game which all were a massive learning experience!
