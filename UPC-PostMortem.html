Ultimate Parkour Challenge Post mortem!

Published 03 June, 2018 by Kienauer Rene

First prototype of the movement system. This was roughly 2 months before the submission deadline.

Download here

Constraints

As part of my games programming education at SAE Vienna we where tasked to develop a game protoype in about 3 months. I had help from one of my collegues and friend Lukas Sch√ºtzenhofer who was the second programmer on this project. The last week before sumbission we got some support from two audio designers who made some sound effects and a dynamic theme. We also got some feedback from Game Designer Alexander Grenus who provided us with his knowledge on game design. Originally we planed to have an animator on the team who would make the needed animations but sadly he was not available so we used free animations provided by Mixamo. Because this was not my first project we wanted do something quite ambitious.

Idea

At the time I was watching a lot of Super Mario 64 Speedruns and was completly amazed how much depth the game had in a profiecent enough players hands. So the inital idea was to do a third person jump and run with an expresive move set that players could use to chain move after move.

Iteration

The first few month were spent in the playground level you see above fleshing out the core move set of the game. Very quickly we had moveset consisting of Run, Jump, Long Jump, High Jump, Wall Dash, Wall Run, Ledge Grab, Dashing and Swinging. One of the hardest things was to get the jumps to feel right. There was an very essential Gamasutra article for us which went in detail how to craft a good jump. We also realized that making a good enough third person camera in our time frame would not be possible, so pivoted our initial idea a little bit and made all our levels as linear as possible.

We started building the hard levels first because at that time we were not sure if our moveset would work in practice. Building the levels involved lots of playtesting by ourself and other people. The tutorials were very hard to craft and nearly every tutorial level was at one point scrapped and rebuild from scratch.

The last thing we iterated quite a bit on was the feel of speed. We realized very late in the project that the feeling of accelerating and speeding through the level were intergral to the players experience. We played around with acceleration curves of the players movement speed, field of view effects and trails on the player.

Challenges

I was quite suprised that so mutch vector math was involved in crafting the players moveset. In nearly every move somehow there is at least on Dot product hiding. After the project I felt quite profiecent in my abilites to do all kinds of vector magic.

We also took a while to realize we have to use a State Machine to be able to transitions between the moves in code else we would have had lots of spaghetti. You have to consider that which move is triggered is greatly influenced in which state the player is currenlty, for example it is only allowed to long jump if the player is sliding currently and presses A.

The animations were not as much a problem as we where anticipating because our animations were triggered via the state machine we did not have to build out the animation state machine and could just play animations when the player did enter a state. Because we did not have all animations we needed we sometimes just took one frame we thought would convey the move the best and did just blend to that frame and freezed the animation frame.

One thing that was actually to hard for us was building an intuitive third person moveset that controlls well. If you try to rotate the character about 180 degrees you can see the issues we have when the player tries to do rapid movements. Luckily we realized that early on and at least we changed the design so I does not get in the way all the time.

The other usual challenge was handling all the code and ensuring that the projects code had at least some consistency and architecture thought behind it. I dont think I can ever be happy with architecture just because you just have to make trade offs. It is not the goal to make clean code, the goal is to make clean enough code and making something fun along the way.

End result

All considered I am quite happy with the end result. We did lots of playtesting on the end of the project and there were quite a lot of people who said they liked it very much. The most often critcism I get is that the controlls feel off and I totally agree with that an believe if we had more time we could have polished that a little more. The second thing people always said that even the tutorials are much to hard, I mostly blame that on suboptimal controlls. World 1 and World 2 were designed with very hard in mind because we wanted to take our moveset to the hardest levels so we can understand how it would work in a SM64 speedrun like context.

Future

I hope that I will find some time and continue work on this project. I truly believe we were on the right track with the moveset we build. Polishing the controlls and replacing the barebones art,animations,sound and levels would greatly benefit the game.
